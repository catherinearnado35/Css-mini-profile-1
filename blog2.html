Soft Tunes, Soft Days

September 30, 2025

"Nothing calms the heart like a familiar melody at bedtime."

I never expected to fall in love with nursery songs as an adult — but here we are. There’s something oddly comforting about the gentle hum of “Twinkle Twinkle Little Star” playing in the background while I sip tea or wind down after a hectic day. The soft, repetitive rhythms work better than most guided meditations I’ve tried.

Sometimes I listen to classic ones, like Brahms' Lullaby, and sometimes I discover modern acoustic versions that feel like a warm hug for the soul. It’s not just about nostalgia — it’s about soothing the chaos inside. I’ve even created a playlist I call “Midnight Calm” that runs for hours, just in case I can't sleep.

These songs were made for children, but I think adults need them too. We all deserve a soft moment in a loud world.

Lullaby on Loop

September 30, 2025
"Five Little Ducks just hit different at 2AM."

I used to laugh when people said they slept with music on — until I tried looping nursery songs one night. Now, I’m the person who knows exactly how many seconds into "Rock-a-bye Baby" I usually fall asleep. It’s a gentle, predictable pattern — no sudden volume jumps, no distracting lyrics, just steady comfort.

Some days, it’s background music while I read. Other days, it's my escape from overthinking. I never thought I’d bond with "The Itsy Bitsy Spider" as an adult, but when everything feels overwhelming, there's real peace in something so small and innocent.

It’s not about being childish. It’s about choosing softness — even if the world keeps pushing us to be hard. Nursery songs are my lullaby of resistance — and I’m okay with that.
